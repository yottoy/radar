Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 20, 2026

Radar ("the app") is a Bluetooth device finder. This policy explains what the app does and does not collect. It's short because Radar collects very little.

The short version

  • Radar does not collect or access your location.
  • Radar has no accounts and asks for no personal information.
  • The only permission Radar requests is Bluetooth, used solely to detect nearby devices and estimate how close you are.

What Radar accesses

Bluetooth. Radar uses your device's Bluetooth to scan for nearby Bluetooth devices and measure their signal strength, so it can show you how close you are. This happens on your device. Radar does not use Core Location and does not access GPS or your location in any form.

Data stored on your device

Radar stores small settings on your device only (such as your free-find status and any device nicknames or icons you set). This information stays on your device and is not transmitted to us. Deleting the app removes it.

Purchases

If you buy a subscription or the Lifetime option, the purchase is processed by Apple. Radar uses RevenueCat to manage and validate purchases. In doing so, RevenueCat processes purchase information and a device identifier to keep your subscription status in sync across reinstalls and to enable "Restore Purchases." This data is used only to provide the purchase functionality; it is not used to track you and is not sold. See Apple's Privacy Policy and RevenueCat's Privacy Policy.

What Radar does NOT do

  • No location collection or tracking.
  • No advertising or ad identifiers.
  • No selling or sharing of personal data.
  • No account, email, or contact information required to use the app.

Children

Radar is not directed at children and does not knowingly collect data from them.

Contact

Questions? Reach us through the Radar support page.
